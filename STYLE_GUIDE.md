# Style Guide

## Voice and Tone
- Write for senior developers. Assume competence — do not over-explain.
- Be direct. Say what you mean in as few words as possible.
- Prefer active voice over passive voice.
- Use second person ("you") when addressing the reader directly.

## Word Choice
- Avoid: simply, just, easy, easily, obvious, obviously, straightforward, seamless, leverage, utilize, robust, scalable, cutting-edge
- Prefer concrete nouns over abstract ones.
- Use the vendor's own terminology for their products. Do not invent nicknames.

## Sentences and Paragraphs
- Maximum sentence length: 35 words. Break long sentences into two.
- Maximum paragraph length: 4 sentences.
- One idea per paragraph.

## Structure
- Use sentence case for all headings (capitalize first word and proper nouns only).
- Do not use more than two levels of nesting for lists.
- Code samples must be in fenced code blocks with a language tag.
- Tables are preferred over bullet lists for comparisons.

## What to Avoid
- Do not make claims without evidence from the research notes.
- Do not editorialize ("This is a great feature"). State facts, let the reader decide.
- Do not use filler transitions ("In conclusion", "To summarize", "It's worth noting").
